# 004 — Quests and Samaritan Status

## Question

Who issues quests?

## Answer (canon)

**Two central quest-giver entities:**

### The Company

Corporate. Builder of hash_kicker template bodies. Issues quests that align with corporate / commercial / structured-world objectives.

- Reward track: **Corporate Samaritan Status**
- Tone: professional, transactional, structured.

### The Admin

The mysterious code-entity (see `002-the-admin.md`). Issues quests aligned with the Cloud's own needs and the Admin's own (opaque) agenda.

- Reward track: **BitRunner Samaritan Status**
- Tone: cryptic, urgent, occasionally unsettling.

---

## Progression rules

- **Each entity has its own quest chain.** Completing quests for an entity rewards its Samaritan Status track and *gradually unlocks more complex quests from that same entity*.
- The two tracks are independent. A runner can pursue both, one, or neither.
- Implication: quest difficulty and complexity are gated by Samaritan Status with the *issuing* entity, not by player level alone.

## In-game implications

- Two persistent quest-log tabs in the menu: "Company" and "Admin".
- Two reputation meters (Corporate Samaritan, BitRunner Samaritan).
- Quest-giving NPCs: The Admin is always menu-accessible; The Company likely operates through depots, terminals, or designated NPCs in-world. (Open question: is there a single Company NPC, or many corporate front-faces?)
- Tonal contrast between the two quest streams should be obvious at a glance — different fonts/colors/glyph density in the terminal UI.
